Formation of Earth

4.6 Billion years ago

Around 4.54 billion years ago, Earth formed from the solar nebula through accretion. Volcanic outgassing likely created the primordial atmosphere and oceans, but oxygen was scarce at that time

Hadeon Eon

4 Billion Years ago

This eon predates any reliable fossil record and lasted until 4.0 billion years ago. During this period, Earth was still molten, and a giant impact with a planet-sized body led to the formation of our Moon.

Archean and Proterozoic Eons

3.4 Billion Years

Life began to emerge during these eons. Microbial mat fossils, like stromatolites, date back to 3.48 billion years ago. Graphite found in 3.7 billion-year-old rocks in Greenland provides early evidence of biogenic substances

Phanerozoic Eon

570 millions years until Present
  • Palaeozoic Era: Arthropods, fishes, and the first land life appeared.
  • Mesozoic Era: Non-avian dinosaurs ruled, followed by their climactic extinction.
  • Cenozoic Era/Today: Mammals rose, and recognizable humans emerged within the last 2 million years
